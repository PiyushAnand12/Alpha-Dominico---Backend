Introduction & Scope

Platform purpose, methodology basis, and output constraints

This specification defines the full technical upgrade of an existing US-only technical screener into a globally-scalable SEPA-based qualification engine. The methodology extracted here is based on structured analysis of principles described in Minervini's published works — not verbatim reproduction — translated into programmatic, threshold-based conditions.

⚠️
Output Constraint (Non-Negotiable): The system output is limited to: "Currently meets SEPA-based qualification criteria." The system must not produce buy signals, entry prices, stop-loss levels, or price targets. All historical performance data is displayed as aggregate statistics only, not as predictive guidance.
✅
Methodology Note: Where the published methodology gives a range rather than a single threshold (e.g., RS rank "80s or 90s"), this document adopts the minimum end of that range as the programmable floor, with Pro users able to adjust upward. All thresholds are flagged clearly as either explicitly stated in the source or as reasonable interpretations of stated principles.

Deliverable 1A — Technical Criteria (Trend Template)

All 8 Trend Template conditions, converted to programmatic rules with thresholds

The Trend Template is the primary technical gate. A stock must satisfy all 8 conditions simultaneously to enter Stage 2 qualification. Failure on any single criterion disqualifies the candidate regardless of fundamental strength.

ID Type Rule Description Programmatic Condition Threshold Fallback Logic
TT-01 MUST Price above both 150-day and 200-day simple moving average price > SMA150 AND price > SMA200 0% margin None. Hard gate.
TT-02 MUST 150-day MA is above 200-day MA (MA alignment confirms uptrend hierarchy) SMA150 > SMA200 0% margin None. Hard gate.
TT-03 MUST 200-day MA has been trending upward for at least 1 month; 4–5 months preferred SMA200_today > SMA200_21daysago (hard minimum)
SMA200_today > SMA200_63daysago (preferred tier)		 21 trading days minimum; 63 preferred If data insufficient, require at least 21-day positive slope. Flag as "early trend" if < 63 days.
TT-04 MUST 50-day MA is above both 150-day and 200-day MAs (full MA stacking) SMA50 > SMA150 AND SMA50 > SMA200 0% margin None. Hard gate.
TT-05 MUST Current price is above the 50-day moving average price > SMA50 0% margin None. Hard gate.
TT-06 MUST Price is at least 30% above its 52-week low (confirms sustained upward momentum) price >= low_52w * 1.30 30% minimum. Best candidates often 100%+. None. Hard gate. Explicitly stated.
TT-07 MUST Price is within 25% of its 52-week high (stock must be near leadership territory) price >= high_52w * 0.75 Within 25% of 52-week high None. Hard gate. Explicitly stated.
TT-08 MUST Relative strength rank vs. benchmark is in the top 30% of all stocks (RS ≥ 70) US: IBD-style RS rank ≥ 70

RS Rank Computation (Proprietary Alternative to IBD)

Since IBD RS data requires a paid licence, the system should compute a proxy RS rank using the following formula (widely used as an approximation):

RS Rank Calculation (Python pseudo-code)
# Weighted 12-month return (emphasises recent performance) def rs_score(closes): q1 = (closes[-1] / closes[-63] - 1) # last 3 months q2 = (closes[-63] / closes[-126] - 1) # 3-6 months ago q3 = (closes[-126]/ closes[-189] - 1) # 6-9 months ago q4 = (closes[-189]/ closes[-252] - 1) # 9-12 months ago return (q1 * 0.40 + q2 * 0.20 + q3 * 0.20 + q4 * 0.20) # Rank score as percentile across all stocks in the market universe rs_rank = scipy.stats.percentileofscore(all_scores, stock_score)
⚠️
India Market Adaptation: For NSE/BSE, the 52-week window references trading days on the respective exchange. Account for exchange holidays independently — do not mix US and India trading calendars. Use exchange-adjusted calendar for each market separately.

Deliverable 1B — Fundamental Criteria

Layer 2 qualification — earnings, sales, margins, ROE, and institutional quality

Fundamental criteria form Layer 2 of the qualification engine. Stocks passing the Trend Template (Layer 1) are evaluated against the following fundamental conditions. The methodology requires earnings and sales growth to be accelerating, not merely positive.

Earnings Per Share (EPS) Rules

ID Type Rule Programmatic Condition Threshold Source Note
EPS-01 MUST Most recent quarter EPS growth vs. same quarter prior year must be strongly positive qEPS_growth_yoy >= threshold ≥ 25% YoY (Pro: adjustable 15–100%) Explicitly stated as key condition; "three out of four top performers" show meaningful Q EPS growth.
EPS-02 MUST EPS growth must be accelerating over the most recent 2–4 quarters (the rate of growth is increasing, not just positive) qEPS_growth_q[-1] > qEPS_growth_q[-2] for at least 2 of last 3 quarter-over-quarter comparisons Acceleration detected in ≥ 2 of last 3 Q comparisons Explicitly stated: "more than 90 percent of biggest stock market winners showed some form of earnings acceleration."
EPS-03 MUST Annual EPS growth (TTM vs. prior year, or fiscal year) must be strongly positive annual_eps_growth_yoy >= threshold ≥ 25% YoY annual (Pro: adjustable 15–100%) Explicitly linked to superperformance in multiple case studies.
EPS-04 SOFT Positive earnings surprise in most recent quarter (beat consensus estimates) reported_EPS > consensus_EPS Any positive surprise; ≥ 5% preferred Stated as important driver of institutional buying; exact threshold is "meaningful." 5% is conservative interpretation.
EPS-05 SOFT Analyst estimate revisions for next quarter / current FY trending upward consensus_revision_30d > 0 Positive revision direction; ≥ 5% revision = strong signal Explicitly stated: revisions of 5%+ linked to better performance.
EPS-06 COND For turnaround situations: current EPS should be materially above prior distressed period recent_2Q_avg_eps_growth >= 100% if prior 4 quarters showed negative or < 10% growth 100%+ YoY for turnarounds (easy comp base) Explicit guidance for turnaround classification in the methodology.

Deliverable 1C — Relative Strength Concepts

Price leadership metrics vs. market and peers

IDTypeRuleConditionThreshold
RS-01 MUST 12-month price performance percentile rank vs. full market universe rs_rank >= 70 70th percentile minimum; 80–90 strongly preferred (explicitly stated)
RS-02 MUST RS rank must be improving over the trailing 3 months (relative trend is up) rs_rank_today > rs_rank_63daysago Positive slope required
RS-03 SOFT During market corrections, the stock declines less than the benchmark index (leadership resilience) stock_drawdown <= benchmark_drawdown * 1.5 Stock should not fall more than 1.5× the general market's correction during same period
RS-04 SOFT Stock is in an industry group that itself is in a leading sector (top 20% of sector RS) sector_rs_rank >= 80 Sector RS 80th percentile or above
RS-05 COND New 52-week high emergence: stock makes new high while market is still below its high (divergence) stock_at_52wk_high AND benchmark < benchmark_52wk_high * 0.95 Positive divergence flag; highly significant if condition met during market correction

Deliverable 1D — Volume Requirements

Institutional accumulation signals via volume analysis

IDTypeRuleConditionThreshold
VOL-01 MUST Up days on volume should exceed down days on volume over trailing 20-day window (accumulation signal) count(up_days_above_avg_vol) > count(down_days_above_avg_vol) over trailing 20 days Majority of high-volume days must be up-days
VOL-02 SOFT Recent trading volume during price advances is above 50-day average volume (institutional participation) avg_vol_on_up_days_10d >= avg_vol_50d * 1.0 Up-day volume ≥ 50-day average
VOL-03 SOFT Volume on pullback/consolidation days is contracting (supply drying up — constructive sign) avg_vol_on_down_days_10d < avg_vol_50d * 0.8 Down-day volume < 80% of 50-day average during consolidation
VOL-04 COND Breakout day volume must be significantly above average (institutional demand confirmation) breakout_day_vol >= avg_vol_50d * 1.40 ≥ 40% above 50-day average volume on the breakout day
VOL-05 COND VCP pivot point: final contraction in base should have at least one day with significantly below-average volume (supply exhaustion) min_vol_in_final_contraction <= avg_vol_50d * 0.50 At least one day at ≤ 50% of 50-day average in the final VCP contraction

Deliverable 1E — Base Structure Concepts (VCP)

Volatility Contraction Pattern detection — quantifiable logic

The VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern) is the most important pattern the methodology describes. It is partially quantifiable programmatically. The following rules capture the measurable aspects.

IDTypeRuleConditionThreshold
VCP-01 SOFT Base duration: stock must be in a consolidation (trading range) of appropriate length base_duration_weeks BETWEEN 3 AND 65 3–65 weeks (explicitly stated range)
VCP-02 SOFT Maximum correction depth within base: from base high to base low base_correction_pct <= max_correction ≤ 35% under normal conditions; ≤ 50% in severe bear markets. Avoid > 60% corrections.
VCP-03 SOFT Contractions (T-count): stock should show 2–4 successive depth reductions in volatility Detect N local peak-to-trough corrections within base where each is ≤ 60% of prior correction depth 2–4 contractions; each ≈ 50% depth reduction from prior (approximate)
VCP-04 SOFT Right-side tightness: price range (H–L) in final 2 weeks of base should be narrow avg_daily_range_pct_final_10d <= avg_daily_range_pct_base_start * 0.50 Final price range ≤ 50% of initial volatility in the base
VCP-05 COND Stock should not be in a late-stage base (base count ≥ 4) — late bases have higher failure rate Count the number of separate consolidation bases since last major breakout; flag if count ≥ 4 Base count ≤ 3 preferred; count ≥ 4 is a warning flag, not a disqualifier
⚠️
VCP Automation Limitation: Full VCP detection requires human pattern recognition for subjective elements (symmetry, shakeout identification). The programmatic version above captures the measurable aspects only. Consider adding a "VCP Signal" flag rather than using it as a hard gate. Manual chart review remains necessary for high-conviction qualification.

Deliverable 1G — Consolidated Rule Matrix

All 38 rules classified by tier, layer, and access level

Mandatory Rules (Hard Gates) — 14 total

All must pass. Any single failure = disqualified.

  • TT-01 through TT-08 (Trend Template)
  • EPS-01 (quarterly EPS growth ≥ 25%)
  • EPS-02 (EPS acceleration)
  • EPS-03 (annual EPS growth ≥ 25%)
  • SLS-01 (quarterly revenue growth ≥ 20%)
  • MGN-01 (margin stable or improving)
  • LQD-01 (minimum volume)
  • LQD-02 (minimum price)
  • VOL-01 (up-day volume dominance)
  • RS-01 (RS rank ≥ 70)
  • RS-02 (RS rank improving)
  • DQ-01 through DQ-08 (disqualifiers)

Optional Rules (Score Enhancers) — 14 total

Add to a composite "confidence score" (1–100). Not hard gates.

  • EPS-04 (positive earnings surprise)
  • EPS-05 (upward estimate revisions)
  • SLS-02 (revenue acceleration)
  • SLS-03 (earnings quality: revenue-driven)
  • MGN-02 (Code 33 pattern)
  • MGN-03 (ROE ≥ 17%)
  • LQD-03 (preferred market cap range)
  • VOL-02 (above-avg volume on up-days)
  • VOL-03 (contracting volume on down-days)
  • RS-03 (market correction resilience)
  • RS-04 (leading sector)
  • VCP-01 through VCP-04 (base structure)

Context-Dependent Rules — 10 total

Applied only when specific conditions are detected (turnaround, breakout day, VCP pivot, etc.):

  • EPS-06 (turnaround EPS requirement)
  • VOL-04 (breakout volume — applied on breakout detection day)
  • VOL-05 (VCP pivot supply exhaustion)
  • RS-05 (divergent new high during market correction)
  • VCP-05 (late-stage base warning)
  • LQD-04 (ETF/REIT exclusion in fundamental layer)
  • DQ-03 (relative drawdown vs. market)
  • DQ-07 (material single-day break)
  • TT-03 (preferred duration applied contextually)

Deliverable 4 — Backend Architecture

Service topology, technology choices, and data pipeline design

🔧Core API Server
  • Runtime: Python 3.11+ with FastAPI
  • Auth: JWT (access) + Refresh tokens; Supabase Auth or custom
  • Rate limiting: Redis-based sliding window per user tier
  • Versioning: /api/v1/ prefix on all routes
  • Docs: OpenAPI/Swagger auto-generated
🔄Screening Pipeline (Async Worker)
  • Runner: Celery workers with Redis broker
  • Schedule: 2 jobs: US (after 4:30 PM ET) + India (after 3:45 PM IST)
  • Parallelism: Batch securities in groups of 500; parallel Celery tasks
  • Monitoring: Flower dashboard; Prometheus metrics
🗄️Data Layer
  • Primary DB: PostgreSQL 15 + TimescaleDB extension
  • Cache: Redis 7 (indicator cache, session, rate limits)
  • Object store: S3 (CSV exports, report PDFs)
  • Search: PostgreSQL full-text search (no external engine needed at v1)
📡Data Ingestion Service
  • Scheduler: APScheduler or cloud cron (AWS EventBridge)
  • Providers: EODHD (price) + Intrinio (US fundamentals) + Screener.in (India fundamentals)
  • Corp actions: Dedicated normalisation pipeline (runs before price ingestion)
  • Backfill: Historical loader for initial database population
📨Alert & Notification Service
  • Email: AWS SES or SendGrid (transactional)
  • Push: Firebase Cloud Messaging (web + mobile)
  • Webhook: User-configurable HTTP endpoints (Pro)

Deliverable 5 — Screening Engine Logic Flow

Step-by-step execution sequence for the daily qualification run

1

Data Validation Gate

Before screening begins, verify: (a) price data for the current date has been fully ingested for the target market; (b) corporate action adjustments have been applied; (c) no price anomalies detected (e.g., split not yet reflected). If validation fails, abort and alert engineering team. Do not run partial screening — partial results are worse than no results.

2

Universe Fetch

Load all active securities for the target market from securities table where is_active = true and security_type = 'EQUITY'. Apply minimum price and volume pre-filters against today's daily_prices to eliminate penny stocks and illiquid names before expensive computation. This reduces the working set by ~40–60%.

3

Indicator Computation (Parallelised)

For each security in the filtered universe, compute and upsert into computed_indicators: SMA-50, SMA-150, SMA-200, SMA-200 lags (21d, 63d), 52-week high/low, average volume 50d, RS score, RS rank (requires all scores computed first for percentile ranking — this is a two-pass step), up/down volume day counts. Use numpy vectorised operations. Run in parallel batches of 500 securities per Celery worker. RS ranking requires a second pass after all raw RS scores are computed to derive percentile ranks.

4

Layer 1: Technical Qualification (Trend Template)

Evaluate TT-01 through TT-08 against computed indicators. Any single failure short-circuits evaluation for that security and logs the failing rule IDs to screening_results.fail_reasons. A stock passing all 8 Trend Template criteria advances to Layer 2. Record tech_score (0–8).

5

Layer 2: Fundamental Qualification

For stocks that passed Layer 1, fetch the 6 most recent quarterly fundamental records from fundamentals_quarterly. Evaluate EPS-01 through EPS-06, SLS-01 through SLS-03, MGN-01 through MGN-03, LQD-03, LQD-04. Apply disqualification rules DQ-04 and DQ-05. Record fund_score (0–10). A stock must pass all mandatory fundamental criteria to receive qualifies = true.

6

Confidence Score Computation

For stocks with qualifies = true, compute an optional criteria confidence score (0–100). Each optional criterion contributes a weighted point value: EPS surprise (8pts), estimate revisions upward (6pts), RS > 80 (+5pts), RS > 90 (+5pts more), code-33 pattern (10pts), ROE ≥ 17% (7pts), VCP signals (up to 15pts combined), volume confirmation (8pts), sector leadership (6pts), market correction resilience (5pts), leading sector (5pts). Weights are configurable in system settings.

7

Change Detection (New Qualifiers / Disqualifications)

Compare today's qualification results against yesterday's results for the same market. Set is_new_qualifier = true where qualifies = true AND yesterday's record shows qualifies = false (or no record exists). Set is_disqualified = true where today qualifies = false AND yesterday qualifies = true. These flags drive the alert engine.

8

Result Persistence & Breadth Update

Bulk upsert all results into screening_results using PostgreSQL INSERT ... ON CONFLICT DO UPDATE. Update market_breadth_daily with aggregate counts. Invalidate Redis cache for dashboard queries. Total runtime target: < 10 minutes for 6,000+ stocks.

9

Alert Queue Population

Push alert events to Redis queue for the notification service. Events: NEW_QUALIFIER, DISQUALIFIED, WATCHLIST_QUALIFIED. The notification service drains this queue asynchronously, preventing alert sending from blocking the screening pipeline.

10

Performance Tracking Update (Async, Next Day)

A separate daily job runs at market open +1 hour. It looks up all qualification events from 5, 20, and 60 trading days ago and computes returns vs. the price on the qualification date. Updates qualification_performance. This job is non-blocking and runs independently of the main screening pipeline.

Alert Engine Flow

1

Event Generation

After screening run completes (Step 9 in engine flow), a dedicated AlertEventGenerator process queries screening_results for all new qualifiers and disqualifications. Cross-references against watchlist_items to generate watchlist-specific events. Pushes event objects to Redis sorted set keyed by priority.

2

User Subscription Resolution

For each event, resolve which users should receive it: query users joined to subscriptions to check tier. Check user's alert preferences (channels enabled, quiet hours, market filters). Build recipient list. For watchlist alerts: only notify users whose watchlist contains that specific security.

3

Message Rendering

Render alert content using templates per channel type. Email: HTML template with logo, list of new qualifiers sorted by confidence score, sector breakdown, disclaimer. Push: Short title + ticker + market. Telegram: Markdown-formatted message (existing format from current screener, extended). Output constraint applies: no price targets, no buy signals.

4

Delivery & Logging

Send via appropriate channel client (SES/FCM/Telegram Bot API/Webhook). On success: log to alert_log with status = 'sent'. On failure: retry up to 3 times with exponential backoff (1m, 5m, 15m). If all retries fail: log status = 'failed' and alert engineering via separate monitoring channel.

⚠️
Alert Batching for Basic Users: Basic tier users receive alerts as a single daily digest rather than real-time per-event notifications. This prevents alert fatigue on days with many new qualifiers, reduces email sending costs, and differentiates Pro value.

Deliverable 9 — Scalability Risks

Identified bottlenecks and mitigation strategies

RiskImpactLikelihoodMitigation
RS Rank Two-Pass Bottleneck
Computing percentile ranks requires all raw RS scores first — sequential dependency prevents full parallelisation		 MEDIUM HIGH Pre-compute raw RS scores in parallel pass 1, aggregate all scores in Redis, compute percentiles in vectorised numpy pass 2 (ms-level operation), then write results. Adds < 30 seconds to pipeline.
Database Write Contention at Pipeline End
Bulk upsert of 6,000+ rows into hypertable while dashboard users are reading		 HIGH MEDIUM Use staging table: write results to screening_results_staging, then atomic RENAME or partition swap. Dashboard reads from prior day until swap completes. Zero-downtime cutover.
External API Rate Limits
EODHD and Intrinio have rate limits that could throttle data ingestion		 HIGH HIGH Implement exponential backoff with jitter. Cache all fetched data — never re-fetch for same date. Distribute calls across time window. Have secondary data source fallback configured. Monitor API quota usage via Prometheus.
Fundamental Data Latency (India)
Indian company results often published late and inconsistently by data providers		 MEDIUM HIGH Cache last-known fundamentals. Flag stocks as "fundamentals pending" if reporting is overdue. Do not downgrade qualification status based on missing data — only change when data is received. Show data age to Pro users.
Alert Volume Spike on High-Activity Days
Bull market breakout days could trigger 200+ new qualifiers — alert volume overwhelms email provider		 MEDIUM MEDIUM Batch alerts per user (not per stock). Implement per-user daily alert cap (e.g., 1 email per user per run, regardless of new qualifier count). Use digest format rather than individual alerts per stock.
TimescaleDB Chunk Accumulation
daily_prices and screening_results grow indefinitely		 LOW HIGH (certain) Configure TimescaleDB compression policies (compress chunks older than 90 days). Implement data retention policies: raw prices: keep all; screening_results: keep 2 years active, archive older; performance_tracking: keep forever (small table).
Multi-Market Timezone Complexity
US (4:30 PM ET) and India (3:45 PM IST) pipelines overlap in UTC — risk of one pipeline affecting the other's resources		 MEDIUM MEDIUM Run US and India screening as completely isolated Celery task queues with separate worker pools. Configure resource limits per queue. India closes at ~10:15 AM UTC, US pipeline starts at ~21:30 UTC — no actual time overlap.

Data quality failures and how to detect and prevent them

RiskConsequence if UndetectedDetection MethodRemediation
Unadjusted Split / Bonus Share Prices
Stock appears to have crashed 50% overnight — it just split		 Stock incorrectly disqualified (fails TT-01 to TT-07); false disqualification alert sent to users Compare raw close with prior day raw close. If change > 40% in either direction, cross-check against corporate action calendar before processing. Flag for manual review. Apply corporate action adjustment retroactively. Stale Fundamental Data
EPS data from 2 quarters ago used as "latest" due to provider delay		 Stock qualifies on outdated (potentially deteriorating) fundamentals; false positive qualification Check fundamentals_quarterly.period_end date. If latest record is > 120 days old, flag stock as "STALE_FUNDAMENTALS". Do not disqualify, but mark in results and alert Pro users. Implement fallback fundamentals provider. Show data staleness indicator in dashboard. Do not suppress qualification — inform users of data age.
Survivorship Bias in Performance Stats
Delisted stocks excluded from historical return calculations, inflating apparent success rates		 Performance statistics appear better than reality; misleads Pro users evaluating methodology quality Maintain securities.is_active = false for delisted stocks but retain all historical data. Include delisted stocks in performance calculations using their final available price as the return endpoint (often near zero for bankruptcies). Retroactively include delisted stocks in performance cohort. Add disclosure to statistics panel explaining survivorship bias treatment.
Lookahead Bias in Historical Screening
When displaying "how this stock looked on date X", accidental use of data that wasn't available on date X		 Historical performance looks unrealistically good; fundamentals data from future quarters used in past screening dates All historical queries must be point-in-time: use report_date <= target_date (not period_end) for fundamental lookups. Enforce strict time filtering in all historical APIs. Add report_date to all historical screening logic. Add integration tests that verify point-in-time correctness using known historical cases.

Data provider fails to deliver prices for one trading day — previous day's data reused accidentally		 Screening runs on stale prices; indicators computed incorrectly; could persist for days if not caught Before every screening run: verify that the count of securities with prices for today's date equals at least 90% of yesterday's count. BSE prices in rupees, NSE in rupees — but some data providers mix crore/lakh units for market cap		 Market cap filter produces wrong results; stocks filtered incorrectly based on unit confusion Normalise all currency/unit fields on ingestion.
